Ontario Climate Data Portal



Data, maps, figures and reports

An open source of climate change information



Maps:

Thousands visually-appealing, informative and interactive web maps based on a big ensemble of climate projection dataset.

Figures

Annual variations of basic climate variables and climate extreme indices tell us how fast the climate is changing at various spatial levels.


Data

Large ensembles of high resolution historical observations and future projected climate data for climate change and impact researches.

Documents

Many PDF formate reports or web pages about special climate change topics including factsheets, present and future climate condition analyses.


FAQ

Answers of some questions about all aspects related to the OCDP including data sources, methodology, products and our future researches.

About

What we found

"Temperature may increase 1.8 and 3.2°C in 2050s, 1.8 and 5.6°C in 2080s under RCP2.6 and RCP8.5, respectively."

"Precipitation may increase 17 and 47mm in 2050s, 12 and 87mm in 2080s under RCP2.6 and RCP8.5, respectively."

"Extreme maximum temperate may increase 1.6°C and 3.3°C in 2050s, 1.6 and 5.8°C in 2080s under RCP2.6 and RCP8.5, respectively."

"Summer days may increase 14 and 27 days in 2050s, 14 and 50 days in 2080s under RCP2.6 and RCP8.5, respectively."

"Extreme minimum temperature may increase 3.0°C and 5.7°C in 2050s, 3.1°C and 10.4°C in 2080s under RCP2.6 and RCP8.5, respectively."

"Frost days may decrease 17 and 27 days in 2050s, 16 and 48 days in 2080s under RCP2.6 and RCP8.5, respectively."

"Heating degree days may decrease 590 and 1000 °Cdays in 2050s, 580 and 1700 °Cdays in 2080s under RCP2.6 and RCP8.5, respectively."

"Maximum 1-day rainfall may increase 0.5 and 1.7 mm in 2050s, 0.2 and 4.3 mm in 2080s under RCP2.6 and RCP8.5, respectively."

"Heavy precipitation days may increase 2 and 3 days in 2050s, 2 and 4 days in 2080s under RCP2.6 and RCP8.5, respectively."
